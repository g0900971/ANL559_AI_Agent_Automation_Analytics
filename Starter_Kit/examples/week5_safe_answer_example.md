# Week 5 Example - Safe Executive Answer

June churn rose from 9.4 percent to 11.8 percent. The increase is concentrated among monthly-plan customers with fewer than five usage sessions in the last 30 days.

A plausible explanation is onboarding friction because support tickets about onboarding delays increased in June. This is not proof of causality.

The evidence does not support saying the May price increase caused the churn increase. The pricing note says existing customers were not moved to the new price in June.

Next test: compare churn among low-usage new customers with and without onboarding-delay tickets.
