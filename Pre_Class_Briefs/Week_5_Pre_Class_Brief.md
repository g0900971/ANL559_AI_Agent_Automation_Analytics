# Week 5 Pre-Class Brief - The Executive Question

## Situation

The CEO asks why churn changed in June. A useful answer must be clear, evidence-based, and careful about uncertainty. Northstar has several internal notes and summaries that may help answer the question.

The business needs an answer that can be sent to an executive audience without overstating what the available evidence shows.

## Stakeholders

The main stakeholders are:

- CEO.
- Analytics lead.
- Customer Success lead.
- Product lead.

They need a concise explanation and a sensible next step.

## Data Available

The following source file is available in `Starter_Kit/sample_data/`:

| File | Description |
|---|---|
| `week5_knowledge_base.md` | Short internal source notes about churn, pricing, support, product incidents, and marketing activity. |

## Source Notes To Read

`week5_knowledge_base.md` contains:

- KPI Pack.
- Pricing Note.
- Support Summary.
- Product Incident Note.
- Marketing Campaign Note.

## Pre-Class Thinking

Before class, write short notes on these questions:

1. What does the CEO likely want to know?
2. Which source notes seem most relevant to the question?
3. What would make an executive answer convincing?
4. What would make an executive answer risky?
5. What follow-up analysis might be needed before making a stronger claim?

## Bring To Class

Bring:

- The Week 5 source note.
- A short draft answer to: "Why did churn rise in June?"
