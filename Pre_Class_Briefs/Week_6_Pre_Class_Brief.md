# Week 6 Pre-Class Brief - Launch Review

## Situation

Northstar Retail is considering whether the analytics work from the previous weeks is ready for use beyond the classroom setting. The launch committee wants a recommendation, not a feature tour.

The team must be prepared to discuss what is ready, what is not ready, what evidence exists, and who would own follow-up actions.

## Stakeholders

The main stakeholders are:

- Launch committee.
- Analytics lead.
- Operations manager.
- Customer Success manager.
- Executive sponsor.

They need a clear recommendation about scope, risk, and ownership.

## Materials Available

Students should bring their work and notes from Weeks 1-5, including any available:

| Material | Description |
|---|---|
| Week 1 reporting work | KPI reporting artefact, definitions, and checks. |
| Week 2 reconciliation work | Notes on conflicting numbers and source comparison. |
| Week 3 retention work | Customer-prioritisation or decision notes. |
| Week 4 internal tool work | Notes on user inputs, outputs, and operating needs. |
| Week 5 executive answer work | Evidence-based answer and source notes. |

## Pre-Class Thinking

Before class, write short notes on these questions:

1. Which part of your work is most ready to use?
2. Which part still needs more evidence?
3. Who would own the work if it were used after class?
4. What should stop the team from releasing something too broadly?
5. What would you say if a stakeholder asks for a stronger claim than the evidence supports?

## Bring To Class

Bring:

- Your weekly artefacts or notes from Weeks 1-5.
- One claim you feel confident defending.
- One claim you are not yet confident defending.
- Two questions you expect the launch committee to ask.
